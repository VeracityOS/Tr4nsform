3. Primary user journeys

Four core user types move through the stack in different ways, but all roads must end in a controlled order, payment, fulfilment, and reconciliation state.

Sovereign individual / patient / client

1. Discover

Arrives via protocols page, itinerary page, referral, partner QR, or direct campaign. Enters through a wallet-first access layer rather than legacy account signup.

2. Connect + profile

Connects Bitcoin-enabled wallet, accepts consent, states goals, symptoms, timing, location, and budget. Profile state is created without forcing legacy KYC for general browsing or pre-qualification.

3. Match + configure

Selects peptide stack, physical protocol, consultation, or retreat. The system assembles products, sessions, prerequisites, and optional upgrades.

4. Pay + book

Pays deposit or full amount via BTCPay / Bitcoin / Lightning / supported stablecoin rails. Booking and order are created atomically.

5. Fulfil + continue

Receives product, attends consult, joins retreat, or starts a guided protocol. Follow-up and reorder logic feed back into the same wallet-linked profile.

Partner / clinic / retreat operator / reseller

1. Onboard

Partner access, pricing tier, territory, permitted services, invoicing terms, and fulfilment rules are set.

2. Source demand

Partner sends clients directly into TR4NSFORM journeys or requests local stock under controlled partner pricing.

3. Order and allocate

Stock is reserved or issued against a partner order, with retail value, discount conceded, and expected payment captured in the ledger.

4. Report and settle

Partner confirms what sold, what remains on hand, and what funds were collected. Settlement or overdue escalation runs off this record.

5. Reconcile and expand

Only reconciled partners keep access to discounted pricing, increased stock limits, and lead routing.

7. Suggested systems blueprint

A practical deployment pattern for TR4NSFORM is to keep the experience and wallet edges modern, while the service ledger and reconciliation core remain explicit and auditable.

Front-end and orchestration

Webflow or custom front end for discovery, wallet-first access page, forms, protocol builder, retreat builder, and partner portal. API layer handles orders, intake, bookings, pricing, and service states.

Payments

BTCPay Server for Bitcoin / Lightning checkout and invoice events. Stablecoin support can be added as a controlled secondary rail, but settlement should still land in the same order and finance model.

Back-office control core

Single order ledger, invoice ledger, stock ledger, and reconciliation queue. This can sit in a CRM / ERP style back office, but the key requirement is one authoritative source for commercial truth across products, services, partners, and finance actions.

Analytics and governance

Dashboards should expose category revenue, open orders, deposits outstanding, partner-held stock, overdue invoices, stock loss, realized margin, conversion by protocol, and retreat economics.

8. Executive operating rules

These rules keep the stack commercially safe while allowing broad service innovation.

No orphan services

Every consult, retreat, protocol, or product shipment must map to an order record and a commercial state.

No invisible discounting

Retail price, partner price, discount conceded, and approver should all be visible for every line item.

No unreconciled partner stock

Partner-held stock without current count, expected remittance, and last-sale confirmation becomes a controlled exception.

Booking follows payment logic

Retreat and service capacity should be tied to deposit or payment rules, not informal promises.

One truth for finance

BTCPay, manual bank receipts, stablecoin transfers, and partner cash all need to land in the same ledger model.

Aftercare drives lifetime value

Follow-up, repeat cycles, memberships, and protocol evolution should feed back into the same wallet-linked service history.
